Arabic Foundations I

Semester Learning Program

Week 1 — Alphabet & Script Foundations

Module Goal

By the end of Week 1, you will confidently recognize and write all isolated forms of the Arabic alphabet, understand the basic sound system, and begin mapping letters to sounds without relying on transliteration. Accuracy is the priority—speed comes later.

Time Structure (Suggested Rhythm)

Total: ~6–8 hours
Four 90-minute sessions + two light review sessions.

Core Outcomes
  • Identify all 28 Arabic letters in isolated form.
  • Pronounce basic consonants with approximate accuracy (focus on ح، خ، ع، ق، ر).
  • Write letters neatly on lined paper from right → left.
  • Understand short vowels, long vowels, and the concept of an abjad.
Required Mediums of Learning
  • Textbook: Alif Baa (Units 1–2)
  • Video: Clear, slow alphabet introduction (YouTube)
  • Audio: Slow alphabet recitation (daily short exposure)
  • Writing: Dedicated Arabic notebook
Study Plan — Block by Block

Block 1 (90 minutes): First Contact

  • Skim Alif Baa Unit 1 to understand goals & structure.
  • Watch a 10–15 minute alphabet introduction video.
  • Select 7–8 letters; write each one 10–15 times.
  • Create a “letter chart” with:
    • Letter shape
    • Name (Latin transliteration)
    • Approximate sound
Teacher-You: Pre-select the video, choose 7–8 letters, and set up notebook sections before the study session begins.

Block 2 (90 minutes): Alphabet Completion

  • Work through the rest of Unit 1.
  • Complete the letter chart with all 28 letters.
  • Record yourself reciting the alphabet slowly.
  • Star the letters that feel hardest.

Block 3 (90 minutes): Vowels Deep Dive

  • Study short vowels (َ ِ ُ) and long vowels (ا ي و).
  • Practice consonant + vowel combinations: بَ بِ بُ · تَ تِ تُ
  • 10-minute listening drill: track alphabet audio on paper.
  • Begin a “sound diary”: describe how difficult sounds feel physically.

Block 4 (90 minutes): Consolidation & Light Reading

  • Rewrite the entire alphabet neatly.
  • Attempt reading a simple chart with no transliteration.
  • 10–15 minutes of flashcards.
  • Write a short reflection on what the script feels like so far.
Micro-Quiz / Checkpoint (End of Week)
  • Write all letters from memory (isolated form).
  • Record a 1-minute audio reciting the alphabet twice.
  • Check your letter chart for accuracy & mark mistakes.
Culture & Context Focus

Research the cultural significance of Arabic script:

  • Arabic’s status as a sacred language in Islamic tradition.
  • Calligraphy as an art form across centuries.
  • Why Arabic script feels visually and spiritually expressive.
  • Differences among Arabic dialects in writing vs. speech.
End-of-Week Reflection
  • Which 3 letters felt easiest? Why?
  • Which 3 sounds felt hardest (and why)?
  • Do you feel less intimidated by the script compared to Day 1?
