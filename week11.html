Arabic Foundations I

Semester Learning Program

Week 11 — Future Tense & Goals

Weekly Focus

Future time with سـ and سوف and talking about plans and ambitions.

Time Structure (Suggested Rhythm)

Total: ~7–9 hours
Three 2-hour grammar/practice blocks + two short drill sessions.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of Week 11, you will be able to:

  • Form simple future sentences using verbs you know.
  • Talk about your goals for study, travel, and career.
  • Recognize future markers in texts.
  • Use سـ and سوف appropriately in context.
Core Input & Resources
  • Textbook lesson on future tense or online grammar resources.
  • Optional: short excerpt from a Saudi or Lebanese writer talking about hopes/dreams.
  • Create: #week11-future-plans — your future tense sentence collection.
Study Plan — Block by Block

Block 1 (120 minutes): Future Tense System

  • Study the future tense markers: سـ and سوف.
  • Understand when to use each form:
    • سـ — attached prefix (more common in speech)
    • سوف — separate word (more formal/literary)
  • Create a conjugation chart converting 5 present-tense verbs to future:
    • أدرس → سأدرس — I will study
    • أذهب → سأذهب — I will go
    • أعمل → سأعمل — I will work
    • أتكلم → سأتكلّم — I will speak
    • أقرأ → سأقرأ — I will read
  • Write 8–10 basic future sentences using your charts.
Teacher-You: Pre-select the grammar resource and prepare your verb list. Focus on verbs you already know from present tense.

Block 2 (120 minutes): Goals & Plans

  • Watch a YouTube beginner lesson on MSA future tense if available.
  • Create a 1-page "Future Plans Verb Bank" with 15–20 goal-related verbs:
    • to travel — سأسافر
    • to learn — سأتعلم
    • to visit — سأزور
    • to graduate — سأتخرج
    • to improve — سأحسّن
  • Write 10 "I will…" sentences about the next week.
  • Write 5 "In the future I want to…" sentences about long-term goals.
  • Record yourself reading your goal sentences aloud.

Block 3 (120 minutes): Integration & Timeline Exercise

  • Read short texts that include future tense markers.
  • Underline all future verbs you recognize.
  • Create a timeline exercise:
    • Take 5 verbs you know
    • Write them in present, past, and future forms
    • Create sentences showing: "Yesterday I…", "Today I…", "Tomorrow I…"
  • Write a clean, polished paragraph: "My Goals for Learning Arabic" (6–8 sentences, mixing present and future).
Active Practice
  • Write 10 "I will…" sentences about the next week.
  • Write 5 "In the future I want to…" sentences.
  • Practice saying your goal sentences out loud as you move through your day.
  • Convert 5 present-tense sentences from previous weeks into future tense.
Assignments

Assignment 1: Goal Paragraph (Arabic)

  • Write 6–8 sentences about your study and travel goals.
  • Include at least 5 future tense verbs.
  • Topics to consider:
    • Where you want to travel
    • What you want to study
    • How you will improve your Arabic
    • Career or personal goals

Assignment 2: Timeline Exercise

  • Create present, past, and future versions of the same 5 verbs.
  • Write a sentence for each tense showing the progression of time.
  • Example format:
    • Past: درست العربية أمس — I studied Arabic yesterday
    • Present: أدرس العربية اليوم — I study Arabic today
    • Future: سأدرس العربية غداً — I will study Arabic tomorrow
Quiz / Checkpoint
  • Identify tense from context: read 10 sentences and mark whether they are present, past, or future.
  • Convert present sentences to future: given 5 present-tense sentences, rewrite them in future tense.
  • Fill-in-the-blank: complete 8 sentences with the correct future form of the verb.
  • Record yourself reading your goal paragraph aloud.
Culture & Reflection

Watch or read about Vision 2030 in Saudi Arabia or a similar development project, and note how Arabic uses future / planning language.

  • How do Arabic speakers express long-term goals and national visions?
  • What vocabulary is used for "planning," "development," and "future"?
  • Reflect in English: "How does thinking about the future in Arabic feel different from English or Spanish?"
End-of-Week Reflection
  • Which future marker (سـ vs. سوف) feels more natural to you?
  • How confident do you feel talking about your goals in Arabic?
  • Can you read your goal paragraph smoothly out loud?
  • What future-tense verb do you find yourself using the most?
  • How does expressing future plans in Arabic compare to your native language?
