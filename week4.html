Arabic Foundations I

Semester Learning Program

Week 4 — Present Tense & Basic Sentences

Module Goal

By the end of Week 4, you will form present-tense sentences using "I," "you (m/f)," "he," "she," and "we," apply them to daily routines, and confidently read/write simple present-tense structures. This is where Arabic starts becoming functional.

Time Structure (Suggested Rhythm)

Total: ~7–9 hours
Three 2-hour grammar/practice blocks + two short drill sessions.

Core Outcomes
  • Understand the root + pattern system for present tense.
  • Correctly conjugate common verbs (to study, to live, to work, to drink, to go).
  • Use present-tense sentences in everyday contexts.
  • Read beginner-level short paragraphs in MSA.
  • Write a simple routine paragraph (5–7 sentences).
Required Mediums of Learning
  • Al-Kitaab Part One, Lesson 2
  • YouTube explanation: Present tense in MSA
  • Verb tables (you will create your own)
  • Audio: Slow MSA examples of daily-routine sentences
  • Notebook for your conjugation charts
Study Plan — Block by Block

Block 1 (120 minutes): The Present Tense System

  • Read Al-Kitaab Lesson 2 grammar notes.
  • Create a conjugation chart for 5 core verbs:
    • يَسْكُن — to live
    • يَدْرُس — to study
    • يَشْرَب — to drink
    • يَذْهَب — to go
    • يَعْمَل — to work
  • Memorize the prefixes for present tense:
    • أَ = I
    • تَ = you (m), she
    • تَ...ين = you (f)
    • يَ = he
    • نَ = we
  • Write 8–10 basic sentences using your charts.

Block 2 (120 minutes): Daily Life Sentences

  • Watch a YouTube beginner lesson on MSA present tense.
  • Create a 1-page "Daily Routine Verb Bank".
  • Expand to 12–15 verbs by adding:
    • to read (يَقْرَأ)
    • to write (يَكْتُب)
    • to eat (يَأْكُل)
    • to speak (يَتَكَلَّم)
    • to listen (يَسْتَمِع)
  • Write 6–8 sentences describing your day.
  • Record yourself reading the paragraph aloud.

Block 3 (120 minutes): Reading & Pattern Recognition

  • Read the mini-texts in Lesson 2 aloud.
  • Underline all present-tense verbs.
  • Create a 2-column list:
    "Verbs I recognize immediately" & "Verbs I pause on."
  • Practice rewriting 2–3 of the texts in your own words.
  • Write a clean, polished paragraph:
    "A Typical Day in My Life" (5–7 sentences).
Micro-Quiz / Checkpoint
  • Conjugate 3 verbs for all pronouns (I, you m/f, he, she, we).
  • Translate 8 present-tense sentences Arabic → English.
  • Translate 8 simple English → Arabic sentences.
  • Record yourself reading your daily routine paragraph aloud.
Culture & Context Focus

Explore how daily routines differ between:

  • Saudi Arabia (e.g., prayer times, social rhythms)
  • Lebanon (e.g., coffee culture, daily greetings)

Note words or phrases that connect routines to cultural identity.

End-of-Week Reflection
  • Which verb patterns feel intuitive? Which still confuse you?
  • How natural do your daily-routine sentences feel?
  • Can you read your paragraph smoothly out loud?
  • What present-tense verb do you find yourself using the most?
