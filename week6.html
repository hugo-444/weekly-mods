Semester Learning Program

Week 6 — Listening & Pronunciation Lab

Module Goal

By the end of Week 6, you will improve your listening skills, distinguish between similar Arabic sounds, understand the gist of very short audio clips, and enhance your pronunciation through shadowing techniques. This week trains your ear and your mouth.

Time Structure (Suggested Rhythm)

Total: ~7–9 hours
Daily practice: 60–90 minutes of focused listening and pronunciation work.
Mix of sound discrimination drills, shadowing practice, and listening comprehension.

Core Outcomes
  • Distinguish between similar Arabic sounds (ح vs ه, ق vs ك, etc.).
  • Understand the gist of very short audio clips related to greetings and routine.
  • Improve accent through shadowing (listen and repeat immediately).
  • Recognize stress patterns in Arabic words.
  • Build confidence in listening to natural MSA speech.
Required Mediums of Learning
  • Audio from Alif Baa/Al-Kitaab or from open courses like MSU's audio
  • Practice listening resources (YouTube "Arabic alphabet pronunciation" and simple dialogues, or beginner MSA listening playlists)
  • Audio bank: Curated set of 5–10 clips (greetings, introductions, daily routine)
  • Shadowing scripts: Transcriptions you type up for shadowing practice
  • Recording device (phone is fine) for self-recording and comparison
Study Plan — Block by Block

Block 1 (120 minutes): Sound Discrimination & Minimal Pairs

  • Create a list of 10 minimal pairs (words that differ by one sound):
    • Examples: حار / هار (hot / warm), قلب / كلب (heart / dog)
  • Listen to each pair 5 times, focusing on the difference.
  • Record yourself saying each pair and check you can hear the difference.
  • Practice distinguishing sounds: ح vs ه, ق vs ك, ع vs أ.

Block 2 (120 minutes): Shadowing Practice

  • Select 3–4 short audio clips from your audio bank (greetings, introductions, routine).
  • For each clip:
    • Listen once without repeating (just absorb).
    • Shadow 3–5 times: listen and repeat immediately, trying to match timing and intonation.
    • Record your shadowing and compare with the original.
  • Type up transcriptions of the clips for reference (your shadowing scripts).
  • Focus on matching rhythm, stress, and flow, not just individual sounds.

Block 3 (120 minutes): Listening Comprehension

  • Listen to 5–7 short audio clips (30 seconds to 2 minutes each).
  • For each clip:
    • Listen once without stopping.
    • Write 2–3 sentences (in English) summarizing what you caught.
    • Listen again and add details you missed.
    • Check against transcript if available.
  • Create a listening log: track which topics you understand best (greetings, routine, introductions).

Block 4 (90 minutes): Stress Patterns & Rhythm

  • Study basic stress patterns in Arabic words (typically on the first or second syllable).
  • Listen to 10–15 words and identify where stress falls.
  • Practice saying words with correct stress.
  • Record yourself and compare with native pronunciation.
  • Review all minimal pairs and shadowing clips from the week.
Micro-Quiz / Checkpoint
  • Listening quiz with multiple choice: "Which word did you hear?" (using minimal pairs).
  • Identify where stress is in 5 given words.
  • Listen to 3 short clips and write 1–2 sentence summaries in English.
  • Record yourself shadowing a 30-second clip and self-assess your pronunciation.
Culture & Context Focus

Watch a short video on regional accents in Arabic:

  • Gulf Arabic (Saudi, UAE, Kuwait)
  • Levantine Arabic (Lebanon, Syria, Jordan, Palestine)
  • Egyptian Arabic
  • How these differ from MSA in pronunciation and vocabulary

Reflect: Which sounds feel most challenging to you? Are there sounds in Arabic that remind you of sounds in Spanish or English? How does listening to different regional accents help you understand the diversity of the Arabic-speaking world?

End-of-Week Reflection
  • Which minimal pairs are still difficult to distinguish?
  • How has shadowing practice affected your pronunciation confidence?
  • What percentage of short audio clips can you understand the gist of?
  • Which listening topics (greetings, routine, introductions) are easiest for you?
  • What did you learn about regional accents that surprised you?
