Arabic Foundations I

Semester Learning Program

Week 5 — Reading Foundations & Short Stories

Module Goal

By the end of Week 5, you will read short MSA texts at a beginner level, build reading stamina, and learn to infer meaning from context. This week focuses on transitioning from isolated words to connected texts.

Time Structure (Suggested Rhythm)

Total: ~7–9 hours
Day 1–2: Very short texts (2–3 sentences).
Day 3–4: Slightly longer texts (6–8 sentences).
Day 5: Quiz + dictation.

Core Outcomes
  • Read short vowelled texts at beginner level.
  • Answer basic comprehension questions in English (or simple Arabic).
  • Recognize familiar verb forms and vocabulary in context.
  • Use context clues to infer meaning of unknown words.
  • Build confidence reading connected Arabic text.
Required Mediums of Learning
  • Free graded reading texts in MSA at beginner level (e.g., Lingua.com's Arabic reading texts)
  • Very short adapted stories by Zakariyya Tamer (1–2 paragraphs in simplified form, if available)
  • Reading pack: PDFs/links to 3–5 short texts with glossaries under each text
  • Notebook for vocabulary logs and reading notes
  • Audio recordings of texts (for dictation practice)
Study Plan — Block by Block

Block 1 (90 minutes): Very Short Texts (2–3 sentences)

  • Select 2–3 very short beginner texts from your reading pack.
  • Practice two-pass reading technique:
    • 1st pass: Just circle what you recognize (words, verbs, pronouns).
    • 2nd pass: Underline new words and guess meaning from context before looking them up.
  • Read each text aloud 3 times, focusing on smooth pronunciation.
  • Create a vocabulary log: copy 5 new words per text with Arabic script, transliteration, and English meaning.

Block 2 (90 minutes): Pattern Recognition in Context

  • Re-read the same texts from Block 1.
  • Identify and list all present-tense verbs you find.
  • Circle all pronouns you recognize.
  • Mark any words that look familiar but you're not 100% sure about.
  • Write 2–3 simple comprehension questions for each text (Who? What? Where?).

Block 3 (120 minutes): Longer Texts (6–8 sentences)

  • Move to slightly longer texts (6–8 sentences) from your reading pack.
  • Apply the same two-pass reading technique.
  • Break longer texts into smaller chunks: read 2–3 sentences at a time.
  • After each chunk, summarize in English what you understood.
  • Add 8–10 new vocabulary items to your log.

Block 4 (120 minutes): Reading Worksheet & Dictation

  • Complete reading worksheet for 2 selected texts:
    • Answer 5–8 comprehension questions per text.
    • Copy 5 new words per text into your personal vocab log.
  • Dictation practice:
    • Play an audio (from your chosen text or read by yourself).
    • Pause after each sentence.
    • Write what you hear in Arabic script.
    • Compare with the original text.
  • Review all vocabulary from the week.
Micro-Quiz / Checkpoint
  • Read an unseen text (3–4 sentences) and answer comprehension questions: Who? Where? When? What is happening?
  • Identify 5 present-tense verbs in a given text.
  • Match 10 vocabulary words from your reading pack to their English meanings.
  • Complete a short dictation (2–3 sentences).
Culture & Context Focus

Read a short article or watch a video about the role of storytelling in Arab culture:

  • Hakawati traditions (traditional storytellers)
  • Oral storytelling in cafes and public spaces
  • The connection between storytelling and preserving cultural memory

Reflect: Note any parallels with Mexican oral traditions and cuentos. How do both cultures use stories to pass down knowledge and values?

End-of-Week Reflection
  • Which reading strategies worked best for you (two-pass reading, chunking, etc.)?
  • How comfortable do you feel reading connected Arabic text now compared to Week 1?
  • What types of words do you still struggle to recognize in context?
  • Did you notice any patterns in the texts that helped you understand meaning?
  • What connections did you make between Arabic storytelling and storytelling traditions you know?
