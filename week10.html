Semester Learning Program

Week 10 — Describing People, Places, and Emotions

Module Goal

By the end of Week 10, you will be able to use basic adjectives correctly with gender/number agreement, describe a person and a place in simple sentences, and express simple emotions. This week adds color, detail, and feeling to your Arabic communication.

Time Structure (Suggested Rhythm)

Total: ~7–9 hours
Three 2-hour grammar/practice blocks + two short review sessions focused on adjectives and descriptions.

Core Outcomes
  • Use basic adjectives correctly with gender/number agreement:
    • Big/small (كبير / صغير)
    • Beautiful (جميل)
    • New/old (جديد / قديم)
    • Tired (تعبان)
    • Happy (سعيد)
  • Describe a person in 6–8 sentences (physical appearance, personality, emotions).
  • Describe a place in 6–8 sentences (room, city, building, etc.).
  • Express simple emotions and feelings in Arabic.
  • Recognize adjective agreement patterns in written and spoken Arabic.
Required Mediums of Learning
  • Al-Kitaab Part One, Lesson 6 (adjectives, agreement)
  • Online grammar resources for adjective agreement in Arabic
  • Short excerpt in simple Arabic plus a translated snippet from Gibran Khalil Gibran to discuss aesthetics and description
  • Photos or images for description practice
  • Notebook for your adjective bank and description gallery
Study Plan — Block by Block

Block 1 (120 minutes): Adjective System & Agreement

  • Read Al-Kitaab Lesson 6 grammar notes on adjectives and agreement.
  • Create an "Adjective Bank" with at least 20–30 adjectives:
    • Physical descriptions: big, small, tall, short, beautiful, handsome
    • Emotions: happy, sad, tired, excited, calm
    • Qualities: new, old, clean, dirty, good, bad
  • For each adjective, note:
    • Masculine form
    • Feminine form
    • Example sentence
  • Make "adjective cards" and physically sort them into categories:
    • Positive/negative
    • Emotions/physical
    • People/places/things

Block 2 (120 minutes): Describing People

  • Choose 1 photo per day (friend, family member, public figure) and write 3–5 Arabic sentences describing them.
  • Practice describing:
    • Physical appearance
    • Personality traits
    • Emotions or mood
  • Draft your "Character Description" assignment:
    • Describe a person you admire in 6–8 sentences
    • Include at least 5 different adjectives
    • Check adjective agreement (masculine/feminine)
  • Read your description aloud and record yourself.

Block 3 (120 minutes): Describing Places

  • Choose photos of places (your room, a street, a building, a city) and write 3–5 Arabic sentences describing each.
  • Create a "Description Gallery" with photos and your Arabic captions.
  • Draft your "Room Description" assignment:
    • Describe your study space or bedroom in 6–8 sentences
    • Use adjectives to describe size, color, condition, atmosphere
    • Practice adjective agreement with feminine nouns (like غرفة — room)
  • Read a short excerpt from Gibran Khalil Gibran (in translation) and note how he describes places and emotions.
  • Revise and polish both your character and place descriptions.
Micro-Quiz / Checkpoint
  • Complete 10 sentences with the correct adjective form (masculine/feminine agreement).
  • Match 5 images to appropriate Arabic descriptions.
  • Translate 8 descriptive sentences from English → Arabic.
  • Read aloud your character description and record yourself.
Culture & Context Focus

Brief reading or video about beauty and modesty norms in Arab cultures.

  • Reflect on how language expresses respect (honorifics, polite exaggeration, etc.).
  • Research how physical descriptions differ in Arabic literature vs. everyday conversation.
  • Learn about cultural concepts of beauty and aesthetics in Saudi Arabia and Lebanon.
  • Compare how emotions are expressed in Arabic vs. English or Spanish.
  • Note how descriptive language reflects cultural values and social norms.
End-of-Week Reflection
  • Which adjectives feel easiest to use? Which are still confusing?
  • How natural does your character description feel when you read it aloud?
  • Do you notice the difference between masculine and feminine adjective forms when listening?
  • What emotions or qualities are easiest for you to express in Arabic?
  • How does adding descriptive language change your ability to communicate in Arabic?
