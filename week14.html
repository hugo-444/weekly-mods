Arabic Foundations I

Semester Learning Program

Week 14 — Integration & Project Work

Weekly Focus

Integrating all skills into a small project and focusing on weak spots. This week you will combine everything you've learned—reading, writing, listening, and speaking—into a cohesive project on a single theme.

Time Structure (Suggested Rhythm)

Total: ~8–10 hours
Three 2-hour project work sessions + one 90-minute revision session + one 90-minute presentation prep.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of Week 14, you will be able to:

  • Combine present, past, and future in connected writing.
  • Read, listen, speak, and write on a single theme.
  • Self-diagnose strengths and weaknesses.
  • Create a cohesive multi-paragraph text using all three tenses.
  • Deliver a short oral presentation in Arabic.
Core Input & Resources
  • Choose one theme for your project:
    • Travel to Lebanon
    • Study in Saudi
    • My life as an Arabic learner
  • Reuse any texts, clips, and vocabulary that match your theme from previous weeks.
  • Create: #week14-project-brief — your project instructions written as if you are the professor.
  • Gather resources:
    • Relevant reading texts from Weeks 5, 9, 10
    • Audio clips related to your theme
    • Vocabulary lists you've built throughout the semester
    • Grammar notes on present, past, and future tenses
Study Plan — Block by Block

Block 1 (120 minutes): Project Planning & Theme Selection

  • Choose your project theme (Travel to Lebanon, Study in Saudi, or My life as an Arabic learner).
  • Create your project brief document:
    • Write instructions as if you are the professor
    • Define the scope and requirements
    • List what must be included (all three tenses, specific vocabulary, etc.)
  • Gather all relevant resources:
    • Reading texts related to your theme
    • Audio/video clips to listen to
    • Vocabulary lists from previous weeks
    • Grammar reference materials
  • Create an outline for your 2–3 paragraph project:
    • Paragraph 1: Past tense (what you did/wanted to do before)
    • Paragraph 2: Present tense (what you are doing now)
    • Paragraph 3: Future tense (what you will do/plan to do)
Teacher-You: Be clear about project requirements. Set realistic expectations for yourself as the student. This is about integration, not perfection.

Block 2 (120 minutes): Drafting & Writing

  • Write your first draft of the integrated project (2–3 paragraphs).
  • Ensure you use:
    • At least 3 past tense verbs
    • At least 3 present tense verbs
    • At least 3 future tense verbs
    • Vocabulary from multiple weeks
    • Connectors and opinion phrases where appropriate
  • Read your draft aloud to check for flow.
  • Identify areas that need improvement or clarification.

Block 3 (120 minutes): Revision & Refinement

  • Revise your draft:
    • Check grammar and verb conjugations
    • Ensure all three tenses are used correctly
    • Improve vocabulary variety
    • Add connectors and transitions
    • Polish sentence structure
  • Read related texts or listen to clips on your theme to gather additional vocabulary.
  • Create a final polished version of your written project.
  • Have a native speaker or teacher review if possible (or self-check against grammar resources).

Block 4 (90 minutes): Presentation Preparation

  • Prepare talking points for your oral presentation (3–4 minutes).
  • Create an outline or script:
    • Introduction (greeting, topic)
    • Main points (key ideas from your written project)
    • Conclusion (summary or future plans)
  • Practice reading your presentation aloud 3–4 times.
  • Time yourself to ensure it's 3–4 minutes.
  • Practice pronunciation of difficult words.
Active Practice
  • Draft → revise → finalize a 2–3 paragraph text on your theme.
  • Practice reading your project aloud daily.
  • Prepare talking points for a short oral presentation.
  • Review grammar notes on all three tenses as you write.
  • Listen to audio clips related to your theme and note new vocabulary.
Assignments

Assignment 1: Integrated Project (Written)

  • Write 2–3 paragraphs on your chosen theme (Travel to Lebanon, Study in Saudi, or My life as an Arabic learner).
  • Requirements:
    • Use past tense at least once (describe what you did/wanted before)
    • Use present tense at least once (describe what you are doing now)
    • Use future tense at least once (describe what you will do/plan to do)
    • Include vocabulary from multiple weeks
    • Use connectors and opinion phrases where appropriate
    • Total: 2–3 well-developed paragraphs
  • Example structure:
    • Paragraph 1: "In the past, I wanted to learn Arabic because..." (past tense)
    • Paragraph 2: "Now I study Arabic every day and I enjoy..." (present tense)
    • Paragraph 3: "In the future, I will travel to Lebanon/Saudi and I will..." (future tense)

Assignment 2: Practice Presentation (Audio/Video)

  • Record a 3–4 minute presentation in Arabic based on your written project.
  • Requirements:
    • Clear introduction (greeting, name, topic)
    • Main content covering key points from your written project
    • Conclusion (summary or future plans)
    • Use all three tenses in your speech
    • Speak clearly, even if slowly
  • It's okay to read from notes, but try to maintain eye contact with the camera.
  • Upload to #week14-oral-presentation.
Quiz / Checkpoint

Self-Assessment Checklist: Rate your confidence (1–5 scale) in each area:

  • Reading: Can I read beginner-level Arabic texts and understand the main idea?
  • Writing: Can I write connected paragraphs using correct grammar and vocabulary?
  • Listening: Can I understand the gist of simple Arabic audio clips?
  • Speaking: Can I express my ideas in Arabic, even if slowly?
  • Vocabulary: Do I have enough words to express my thoughts on familiar topics?
  • Grammar: Can I use present, past, and future tenses correctly?

After rating, identify your 2 strongest areas and 2 areas that need the most improvement.

Culture & Reflection

Reflect in English: "If I study Arabic for another year, what would I like to be able to do in Lebanon/Saudi/another Arabic-speaking place?"

  • Consider practical goals:
    • Conversational fluency in a specific dialect
    • Reading newspapers or literature
    • Watching films without subtitles
    • Making local friends
    • Professional or academic use
  • Think about cultural immersion:
    • Understanding cultural references and humor
    • Participating in local traditions
    • Appreciating poetry and literature in the original
  • Write 5–10 sentences about your future Arabic learning goals and how they connect to your cultural interests.
End-of-Week Reflection
  • How did it feel to combine all three tenses in one text?
  • Which tense was easiest to use? Which was most challenging?
  • What vocabulary gaps did you notice while writing your project?
  • How confident do you feel about your oral presentation?
  • What are your 2 strongest skills in Arabic right now?
  • What are 2 areas you want to focus on before the final exam?
  • How has your ability to express yourself in Arabic changed since Week 1?
  • What theme did you choose for your project, and why?
