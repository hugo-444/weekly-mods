Week 8 — Past Tense & Simple Narratives

Module Goal

By the end of Week 8, you will be able to conjugate 5–7 high-frequency verbs in the past tense, tell a simple story about a past day or event, and recognize past vs. present verbs in text. This week builds on your present tense knowledge and opens the door to narrating experiences.

Time Structure (Suggested Rhythm)

Total: ~7–9 hours
Three 2-hour grammar/practice blocks + two short drill sessions focused on past tense patterns.

Core Outcomes
  • Conjugate at least 5–7 high-frequency verbs in the past tense (I, you m/f, he, she, we).
  • Tell a simple story about a past day or event in 6–8 sentences.
  • Recognize past vs. present verbs in written and spoken Arabic.
  • Convert present tense sentences to past tense accurately.
  • Write a narrative paragraph about a past experience (weekend, trip, event).
Required Mediums of Learning
  • Al-Kitaab Part One, Lesson 4 (past tense verbs, pronouns)
  • Online grammar resources for past tense conjugation patterns
  • Optional: Very short excerpt from Hanan al-Shaykh in simplified Arabic (even 2–3 sentences)
  • Audio: Slow MSA examples of past tense narratives
  • Notebook for your past tense conjugation charts and mini-stories
Study Plan — Block by Block

Block 1 (120 minutes): The Past Tense System

  • Read Al-Kitaab Lesson 4 grammar notes on past tense.
  • Create a conjugation chart for 5–7 core verbs in past tense:
    • كَتَب — wrote
    • ذَهَب — went
    • دَرَس — studied
    • أَكَل — ate
    • عَمِل — worked
    • شَرِب — drank
    • تَكَلَّم — spoke
  • Memorize the past tense suffixes:
    • تُ = I
    • تَ = you (m)
    • تِ = you (f)
    • َ = he
    • تْ = she
    • نا = we
  • Compare past tense forms with present tense forms you learned in Week 4.

Block 2 (120 minutes): Converting Present to Past

  • Take your Week 4 daily routine paragraph and convert all present tense verbs to past tense.
  • Write 5 "yesterday I…" sentences each day this week.
  • Create a side-by-side comparison chart: present tense vs. past tense for the same verbs.
  • Practice reading past tense sentences aloud and record yourself.
  • Listen to slow MSA audio examples of past tense narratives.

Block 3 (120 minutes): Writing Simple Narratives

  • Read 2 sample mini-stories with glossaries (create your own or find simplified texts).
  • Underline all past tense verbs in the stories.
  • Draft your "Weekend Story" (6–8 sentences about last weekend).
  • Revise your story, checking verb conjugations and sentence structure.
  • Write a clean, polished version of your narrative paragraph.
  • Record yourself reading your story aloud.
Micro-Quiz / Checkpoint
  • Identify whether a verb is present or past in 10 given sentences.
  • Conjugate 3 verbs fully in past tense (I, you m/f, he, she, we).
  • Convert 5 present tense sentences to past tense.
  • Read aloud a short past tense narrative and record yourself.
Culture & Context Focus

Read or watch a short story set in Lebanon or Saudi Arabia (English or subtitled).

  • Summarize the story in English and pick 3 key Arabic words you want to learn from it.
  • Note how past tense is used to tell personal and cultural narratives.
  • Reflect on how storytelling traditions in Arab cultures compare to Mexican oral traditions.
  • Research the role of personal narratives in Arabic literature and daily conversation.
End-of-Week Reflection
  • Which past tense patterns feel intuitive? Which still confuse you?
  • How natural does your weekend story feel when you read it aloud?
  • Can you easily distinguish past from present verbs in text now?
  • What past tense verb do you find yourself using the most?
  • How does telling stories in past tense change your relationship with Arabic?
