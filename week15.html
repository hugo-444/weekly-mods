Week 15 — Final Exam & Oral Presentation

Module Goal

Week 15 is your summative assessment and celebration of progress. By completing the final exam and oral presentation, you will demonstrate your mastery of the semester's content: reading, writing, listening, and speaking in Modern Standard Arabic. This week is both an assessment and a reflection on your journey as an Arabic learner.

Time Structure (Suggested Rhythm)

Total: ~8–10 hours
Two 3-hour exam sessions (reading/writing, listening) + one 2-hour session for oral presentation preparation and recording.

Core Outcomes
  • Read and comprehend a longer text (12–15 sentences) using familiar vocabulary from the semester.
  • Write a new topic paragraph demonstrating mastery of present, past, and future tenses.
  • Understand and respond to 3–4 listening clips with comprehension questions.
  • Deliver a final recorded presentation (5 minutes) in Arabic covering multiple topics from the semester.
  • Reflect on your learning journey and plan for continued study.
Required Mediums of Learning
  • Review materials from all previous weeks (verb charts, vocab lists, reading texts)
  • Your completed assignments and notes from Weeks 1–14
  • Audio recording device or software for your oral presentation
  • Quiet space for exam completion (reading, writing, listening sections)
  • Access to exam instructions and answer submission platform
Final Exam Components

The final exam follows the same structure as the midterm but with more advanced content. Complete all sections and submit according to the instructions.

Section 1: Reading Comprehension

  • One longer text (12–15 sentences) using familiar vocabulary from the semester.
  • Comprehension questions covering:
    • Main ideas and details
    • Vocabulary in context
    • Inference and interpretation
  • Time allocation: 45–60 minutes

Section 2: Writing

  • Write a new topic paragraph on one of the following themes:
    • Self-introduction (expanded from Week 3)
    • Daily routine (expanded from Week 4)
    • Travel experience (using Week 9 vocabulary)
    • Opinion piece (using Week 12 structures)
  • Requirements:
    • 8–10 sentences minimum
    • Use present, past, and future tenses at least once each
    • Include descriptive language (adjectives from Week 10)
    • Demonstrate proper grammar and vocabulary usage
  • Time allocation: 60–75 minutes

Section 3: Listening Comprehension

  • 3–4 audio clips with comprehension questions.
  • Topics may include:
    • Greetings and introductions
    • Daily routines and activities
    • Travel situations
    • Opinions and preferences
  • Each clip will be played 2–3 times.
  • Time allocation: 45–60 minutes

Section 4: Speaking (Oral Presentation)

  • Final recorded presentation: 5 minutes (if possible, or 3–4 minutes minimum).
  • Your presentation should include:
    • Greeting and introduction
    • Brief self-introduction
    • Description of your daily routine or a past experience
    • Future goals related to Arabic learning or travel
    • Closing remarks
  • You may read from notes, but aim for natural delivery.
  • Submit audio or video recording.
  • Time allocation: 2 hours (preparation + recording)
Teacher-You: Set up clear submission instructions for each section. Create a dedicated space for exam materials and answer uploads. Allow yourself adequate time between sections if needed.
Exam Preparation — Review Checklist

Before the Exam

  • Review all verb charts (present, past, future tenses)
  • Revisit vocabulary lists from all weeks
  • Practice reading texts from Weeks 5, 8, 10, 12
  • Listen to audio clips from previous weeks
  • Review grammar points: pronouns, adjectives, verb conjugations
  • Practice writing paragraphs on different topics
  • Prepare talking points for your oral presentation

During the Exam

  • Read all instructions carefully
  • Manage your time effectively across all sections
  • For reading: read the text twice before answering questions
  • For writing: plan your paragraph before writing, then revise
  • For listening: take notes while listening, use all available replays
  • For speaking: practice your presentation once before recording
Final Exam Submission
  • Complete all four sections: Reading, Writing, Listening, Speaking
  • Submit written answers for Reading, Writing, and Listening sections
  • Upload your oral presentation audio or video file
  • Ensure all submissions are clearly labeled with your name and section
  • Submit by the deadline indicated in your exam instructions

Exam instructions and answer uploads can be found at: #week15-final-exam and #week15-oral-presentation

Culture & Final Reflection

Write a final reflection essay in English (or bilingual if you can) addressing the following prompt:

"What does learning Arabic mean to me as a Mexican learner connected to Durango/Guerrero/Morelos and drawn to Saudi/Lebanese culture? How can I continue on my own after this semester?"

Consider including:

  • Your personal connection to Arabic language and culture
  • How your Mexican heritage intersects with your interest in Arab cultures
  • Specific cultural elements from Saudi Arabia and Lebanon that drew you to Arabic
  • Your progress from Week 1 to Week 15
  • Challenges you overcame and skills you developed
  • Your plan for continued Arabic study beyond this course
  • Goals for using Arabic in travel, work, or personal relationships
  • Resources and strategies for independent learning
End-of-Semester Reflection
  • What was your biggest achievement this semester?
  • Which week or topic was most challenging, and how did you overcome it?
  • What surprised you most about learning Arabic?
  • How has your perception of Arabic language and culture changed since Week 1?
  • Which skills (reading, writing, listening, speaking) do you feel strongest in? Which need more practice?
  • What cultural connections did you discover between Mexican and Arab cultures?
  • How will you continue your Arabic learning journey?
  • What would you tell someone just starting to learn Arabic?
Congratulations!

You have completed 15 weeks of intensive Arabic study. This is a significant achievement. Whether you're continuing with Arabic or taking a break, you've built a strong foundation in Modern Standard Arabic and gained cultural insights into Saudi and Lebanese societies.

Remember: language learning is a journey, not a destination. Keep practicing, stay curious, and continue exploring the rich cultures of the Arabic-speaking world.
