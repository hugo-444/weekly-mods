Arabic Foundations I

Semester Learning Program

Week 7 — Midterm Review & Exam

Module Goal

By the end of Week 7, you will consolidate all material from Weeks 1–6, create comprehensive review materials, and complete a midterm exam that assesses your reading, writing, listening, and speaking skills. This week celebrates your progress and identifies areas for continued growth.

Time Structure (Suggested Rhythm)

Total: ~8–10 hours
Days 1–3: Review and consolidation (2–3 hours each day).
Day 4: Practice exam and final review.
Day 5: Midterm exam (2–3 hours).

Core Outcomes
  • Revisit and master all verb charts, vocab lists, and reading texts from Weeks 1–6.
  • Create a Master Summary Sheet covering alphabet, vowels, sun/moon letters, pronouns, and present tense.
  • Demonstrate comprehension through reading, writing, listening, and speaking assessments.
  • Self-assess strengths and areas needing more practice.
  • Reflect on your learning journey and cultural connections.
Required Mediums of Learning
  • All materials from Weeks 1–6 (notebooks, vocab lists, verb charts, reading texts)
  • Master Summary Sheet template (you will create this)
  • Practice exam questions (you will create these)
  • Recording device for speaking section
  • Quiet space for exam completion
Study Plan — Block by Block

Block 1 (120 minutes): Master Summary Sheet Creation

  • Create a comprehensive Master Summary Sheet covering:
    • Alphabet: All 28 letters with names and sounds
    • Vowels: Short vowels (َ ِ ُ) and long vowels (ا ي و)
    • Sun/Moon Letters: Complete list with examples
    • Pronouns: All personal pronouns (أنا, أنتَ, أنتِ, هو, هي, نحن, etc.)
    • Present Tense: Verb conjugation patterns and prefixes (أَ, تَ, يَ, نَ)
    • Key Phrases: Greetings, introductions, daily routine phrases
  • Use this sheet as your primary study reference.

Block 2 (120 minutes): Vocabulary & Reading Review

  • Review all vocabulary lists from Weeks 1–6:
    • Alphabet and basic sounds
    • Greetings and introductions
    • Daily routine verbs
    • Reading texts vocabulary
  • Re-read 2–3 key reading texts from Week 5.
  • Practice answering comprehension questions.
  • Create flashcards for any words you still struggle with.

Block 3 (120 minutes): Grammar & Writing Review

  • Review all verb conjugation charts (present tense).
  • Practice writing:
    • Self-introduction paragraph (from Week 3)
    • Daily routine paragraph (from Week 4)
  • Write 5–7 new sentences combining greetings, introductions, and routine.
  • Review sun/moon letter rules and practice with examples.

Block 4 (120 minutes): Listening & Speaking Review

  • Re-listen to key audio clips from Weeks 3, 4, and 6.
  • Practice shadowing your introduction and routine paragraphs.
  • Record yourself:
    • Greeting someone
    • Introducing yourself
    • Describing your daily routine
  • Compare your recordings with earlier attempts (if available) to notice improvement.

Block 5 (90 minutes): Practice Exam

  • Complete a practice exam covering all four sections:
    • Reading: 1 short text with comprehension questions
    • Writing: 2 short paragraphs
    • Listening: 2–3 short clips with questions
    • Speaking: 2–3 minute recording
  • Self-grade and identify weak areas.
  • Focus final review on those areas.
Midterm Exam Components

1. Reading Section

  • 1 short text (8–10 sentences) with comprehension questions
  • Questions in English covering: Who? What? Where? When?
  • Identify 5 present-tense verbs in the text

2. Writing Section

  • Two short paragraphs:
    • Self-introduction (4–6 sentences)
    • Daily routine (6–8 sentences, all present tense)
  • Use vocabulary and grammar from Weeks 1–6

3. Listening Section

  • 2–3 short audio clips (30 seconds to 2 minutes each)
  • Multiple choice questions: "Which word did you hear?"
  • Comprehension questions: Summarize what you understood

4. Speaking Section

  • Record a 2–3 minute audio covering:
    • Greeting
    • Self-introduction (name, where you're from, what you study/do)
    • Your daily routine (3–4 sentences)
  • Speak clearly and at a natural pace
Culture & Context Focus

Write a short reflection (in English, 5–10 sentences) on the following prompt:

"How has your perception of Arabic changed since Week 1? What do you now notice in Arabic signs, people's names, or news headlines? How does learning Arabic connect to your interest in Saudi and Lebanese culture?"

Consider:

  • What surprised you about the Arabic script and language?
  • How has your understanding of Arab cultures evolved?
  • What connections do you see between Arabic and your own cultural background?
End-of-Week Reflection
  • Which areas felt strongest on the exam? (reading, writing, listening, speaking)
  • Which areas need more practice in the second half of the semester?
  • How confident do you feel with the alphabet and basic reading now?
  • What progress have you made in speaking and listening since Week 1?
  • What are your goals for Weeks 8–15?
  • How has learning Arabic impacted your understanding of Saudi and Lebanese culture?
