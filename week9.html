Week 9 — Travel Arabic & Survival Phrases

Module Goal

By the end of Week 9, you will be able to ask for directions, prices, and basic help in MSA or simple spoken Arabic, recognize signs and common travel words, and role-play simple travel situations. This week makes Arabic functional for real-world travel scenarios.

Time Structure (Suggested Rhythm)

Total: ~7–9 hours
Three 2-hour practice blocks + two short review sessions focused on travel phrases and dialogues.

Core Outcomes
  • Ask for directions, prices, and basic help in MSA or simple spoken Arabic.
  • Recognize common travel words and signs in Arabic script.
  • Role-play simple travel situations (airport, hotel, taxi, restaurant, café).
  • Use essential phrases for:
    • Airports: check-in, boarding, luggage
    • Hotels: reservations, check-in, room requests
    • Directions: asking where, how far, left/right
    • Taxis: destinations, prices, payment
    • Restaurants: ordering, asking about food, paying
  • Write and perform a travel dialogue script.
Required Mediums of Learning
  • Your textbook's travel chapter if available (Al-Kitaab or similar)
  • Online phrases lists and dialogues (free MSA travel phrase guides, beginner travel videos)
  • YouTube videos: "Arabic Travel Phrases" or "Survival Arabic"
  • Audio: Travel dialogues from language learning apps or courses
  • Notebook for your curated travel phrase list and dialogue scripts
Study Plan — Block by Block

Block 1 (120 minutes): Building Your Travel Kit

  • Gather travel phrases from your textbook and online resources.
  • Create a curated phrase list organized by situation:
    • Airport phrases
    • Hotel phrases
    • Directions and transportation
    • Restaurant and café phrases
    • Emergency and help phrases
  • Write each phrase with:
    • Arabic script
    • Transliteration
    • English meaning
  • Practice saying all travel phrases from your kit once daily.
Teacher-You: Pre-select the most useful phrases and organize them before your study session. Focus on phrases you would actually use in Lebanon or Saudi Arabia.

Block 2 (120 minutes): Writing Travel Dialogues

  • Script 3 mini dialogues yourself:
    • Dialogue 1: Airport check-in
    • Dialogue 2: Taxi ride
    • Dialogue 3: Restaurant ordering
  • Each dialogue should have 2 characters and 8–12 lines.
  • Write one mini travel scenario per day (airport, taxi, check-in, café).
  • Practice reading both roles aloud.
  • Record yourself performing the dialogues (you can play both roles).

Block 3 (120 minutes): Sign Recognition & Real-World Practice

  • Collect photos/screenshots of Arabic signs (online or in your city) and try to read them.
  • Create a "Sign Hunt" assignment:
    • Find 5–10 Arabic signs (restaurant names, street signs, airport signs, etc.)
    • Try to read and translate them
    • Note which words you recognize
  • Watch travel vlogs in Riyadh, Jeddah, Beirut, or another Arab city.
  • Write notes on vocabulary you hear repeatedly in travel contexts.
  • Practice your travel role-play script with a friend or record both roles yourself.
Micro-Quiz / Checkpoint
  • Matching: phrase in Arabic → English meaning (15 travel phrases).
  • Fill-in-the-blank for missing travel words in 5 sentences.
  • Read aloud your travel dialogue script and record yourself.
  • Identify the meaning of 5 Arabic signs or place names.
Culture & Context Focus

Watch a travel vlog in Riyadh, Jeddah, Beirut, or another Arab city and write notes on vocabulary you hear repeatedly.

  • Note cultural differences in travel interactions (tipping, greetings, bargaining).
  • Research how to navigate public transportation in major Arab cities.
  • Learn about cultural etiquette in hotels, restaurants, and taxis in Saudi Arabia and Lebanon.
  • Compare travel experiences: what would be similar or different from traveling in Mexico or the US?
End-of-Week Reflection
  • Which travel phrases feel most useful for your future travel plans?
  • How confident do you feel asking for directions or ordering food in Arabic?
  • Which travel situation (airport, hotel, restaurant) feels most challenging?
  • What cultural differences did you notice in the travel vlogs you watched?
  • If you were traveling to Lebanon or Saudi Arabia next month, what phrases would you prioritize?
