Arabic Foundations I

Semester Learning Program

Week 2 — Vowels, Hamza & Pronouns

Module Goal

By the end of Week 2, you will be able to read and write Arabic letters in connected form, understand short and long vowels with confidence, identify hamza in its common positions, and use basic personal pronouns to create your first simple sentences.

Time Structure (Suggested Rhythm)

Total: ~7–9 hours
Four 90-minute sessions + two short review sessions focused on reading & pronunciation.

Core Outcomes
  • Read and write letters in initial, medial, and final positions.
  • Understand hamza (ء) and recognize it in common placements.
  • Use core pronouns: أنا, أنتَ, أنتِ, هو, هي, نحن, أنتم.
  • Pronounce 5–10 simple words from connected script.
  • Create simple identity statements (“I am…,” “You are…,” “He is from…”).
Required Mediums of Learning
  • Alif Baa, Units 3–4
  • A clear YouTube explanation of connected Arabic script
  • Audio files from Alif Baa (or slow-reading videos)
  • Notebook + pen for structured writing drills
  • Optional: Duolingo or Memrise for pronoun exposure
Study Plan — Block by Block

Block 1 (90 minutes): Connected Forms

  • Read Alif Baa on letter connections (Unit 3).
  • Create a three-column chart:
    • Initial form
    • Medial form
    • Final form
  • Practice 12–15 letters in all 3 positions, writing each 6–8 times.
  • Watch 1 video showing connected Arabic handwriting in real time.
Teacher-You: Pre-select the handwriting video and decide which letters you will focus on today. Keep your chart neat; you will add to it all week.

Block 2 (90 minutes): Vowels & Hamza Deep Dive

  • Study short vowels َ ِ ُ and long vowels ا, ي, و.
  • Create a “vowel grid” using 5 consonants × 3 vowels.
  • Write 10–12 words containing hamza; practice identifying its position.
  • Write your own summary explaining hamza in your own words.

Block 3 (90 minutes): Pronouns & Simple Sentences

  • Copy and memorize the core pronoun set.
  • Create 2–3 identity sentences for each pronoun, e.g.:
    • أنا طالب — I am a student.
    • أنتَ من شيكاغو — You (m.) are from Chicago.
  • Read all sentences aloud and record yourself.
  • Compare your pronunciation to slow MSA recordings.

Block 4 (90 minutes): Integration & Practice

  • Write 12–15 mixed words:
    • Some with hamza
    • Some with tricky connections
    • Some with long vowels
  • Do a self-dictation:
    • Play an Arabic word
    • Pause the audio
    • Write it in connected script
  • Create a mini 10-question quiz for yourself.
Micro-Quiz / Checkpoint
  • Write all pronouns from memory & translate.
  • Write 8 letters in all three positions (initial/medial/final).
  • Read and pronounce 5 vowelled words aloud.
  • Identify hamza correctly in 5 different words.
Culture & Context Focus

Research how pronoun usage reflects politeness in Arab societies:

  • When to use حضرتك in dialects vs. MSA.
  • Plural “you” and its social context.
  • Differences between Saudi and Lebanese address forms.
  • Cultural expectations around formality.
End-of-Week Reflection
  • Which letter connections felt natural? Which felt confusing?
  • Which pronoun do you default to most when thinking in Arabic?
  • What do you still find unclear about hamza?
