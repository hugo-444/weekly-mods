Arabic Foundations I

Semester Learning Program

Week 3 — Basic Vocabulary & Introductions

Module Goal

By the end of Week 3, you will be able to introduce yourself clearly in Modern Standard Arabic, ask for someone’s name, origin, and wellbeing, and hold a basic beginner conversation. You also begin developing your personal phrase bank and first functional vocabulary set.

Time Structure (Suggested Rhythm)

Total: ~7–9 hours
Three 2-hour sessions + two 30-minute review/practice sessions.

Core Outcomes
  • Introduce yourself with name, nationality, where you live, and what you do.
  • Ask and answer basic conversation questions:
    • ما اسمك؟ — What is your name?
    • من أين أنت؟ — Where are you from?
    • كيف حالك؟ — How are you?
  • Recognize and use 30–40 common vocabulary items from Lesson 1 of Al-Kitaab.
  • Write a short introduction paragraph (with optional vowels).
  • Follow slow, beginner-friendly audio dialogues.
Required Mediums of Learning
  • Al-Kitaab Part One, Lesson 1
  • YouTube lesson on “Introducing Yourself in MSA”
  • Audio dialogues (Al-Kitaab or similar slow MSA recordings)
  • Notebook for your personal phrase bank
  • Voice recordings (your phone is fine)
Study Plan — Block by Block

Block 1 (120 minutes): Vocabulary & Key Phrases

  • Read Lesson 1 (English explanations first).
  • Create a two-page spread in your notebook titled: “Self-Introduction Vocabulary”.
  • List 30–40 core vocab items with:
    • Arabic script
    • Transliteration
    • English meaning
  • Highlight all introduction-related expressions.
  • Practice reading the phrases aloud 5–6 times.

Block 2 (120 minutes): Write & Record Your Introduction

  • Draft your introduction (4–6 sentences). Suggested template:
    • اسمي ... — My name is …
    • أنا من ... — I am from …
    • أسكن في ... — I live in …
    • أنا طالب / طالبة — I am a student.
  • Record yourself reading it slowly.
  • Listen back and compare with a native MSA recording.
  • Rewrite your paragraph more cleanly and confidently.

Block 3 (120 minutes): Listening & Dialogue Practice

  • Listen to 1–2 short Al-Kitaab dialogues.
  • Replay each one 3–4 times.
  • Transcribe what you understand (even partial words).
  • Check accuracy with the text in the book.
  • Write your own mini-dialogue:
    “You meet someone from Saudi Arabia or Lebanon for the first time.”
  • Act it out aloud, switching roles.
Micro-Quiz / Checkpoint
  • Write your introduction from memory.
  • Translate 10 vocabulary items from Arabic → English.
  • Read aloud a short dialogue or your self-written conversation.
  • Record yourself asking & answering each intro question.
Culture & Context Focus

Research greeting etiquette in Saudi Arabia and Lebanon.

  • The difference between formal and informal greetings.
  • Handshakes vs. cheek kisses.
  • How much small talk is expected in first meetings.
  • Common opening phrases beyond “مرحبا”.
End-of-Week Reflection
  • How natural does your introduction feel?
  • Does speaking aloud feel more comfortable this week?
  • Which vocabulary items do you consistently forget?
  • Do you notice differences in Saudi vs. Lebanese greetings?
