Arabic Foundations I

Semester Learning Program

Week 12 — Opinions, Feelings & Simple Argument

Weekly Focus

Expressing likes, dislikes, preferences, and simple reasons. Building the ability to articulate opinions and connect ideas with basic connectors.

Time Structure (Suggested Rhythm)

Total: ~7–9 hours
Three 2-hour practice blocks + two short review sessions focused on opinion expression.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of Week 12, you will be able to:

  • Say what you like/dislike and why.
  • Use "because" (لأن) and simple connectors.
  • Write a short opinion paragraph.
  • Express basic emotions and preferences in context.
  • Connect cause and effect in simple sentences.
Core Input & Resources
  • Short beginner texts on hobbies, food, or study preferences (you can reuse Lingua.com style reading texts or similar).
  • One short poem or line by Mahmoud Darwish in Arabic with English translation, to see emotional language in context.
  • Create: #week12-opinion-bank — sentence starters: أحب... (I like...), لا أحب... (I do not like...), لأن... (because...).
Study Plan — Block by Block

Block 1 (120 minutes): Opinion Phrases & Connectors

  • Study key opinion expressions:
    • أحب — I like
    • لا أحب — I don't like
    • أفضل — I prefer
    • أعتقد — I think
    • أشعر — I feel
  • Learn the connector لأن (because) and practice using it.
  • Create your opinion bank with 15–20 sentence starters.
  • Write 10 "I like X because Y" sentences on different topics.
Teacher-You: Pre-select opinion texts and prepare your connector vocabulary list. Focus on topics you care about to make practice meaningful.

Block 2 (120 minutes): Theme-Based Opinion Practice

  • Each day choose one theme and write 5 "I like X because Y" sentences:
    • Food (أحب الطعام العربي لأن...)
    • Music (أحب الموسيقى لأن...)
    • Study methods (أفضل الدراسة في الصباح لأن...)
    • Travel (أحب السفر إلى لبنان لأن...)
  • Read beginner texts on hobbies and preferences.
  • Underline all opinion expressions and connectors you find.
  • Practice reading your opinion sentences aloud.

Block 3 (120 minutes): Emotion Diary & Opinion Paragraph

  • For 5 days, write 2 sentences in Arabic about how you feel and why:
    • Use emotion vocabulary: سعيد (happy), حزين (sad), متعب (tired), متحمس (excited)
  • Read a short poem or line by Mahmoud Darwish in Arabic with translation.
  • Note emotional language and imagery used.
  • Write a clean, polished opinion paragraph (8–10 sentences) on a topic you care about:
    • Why you want to learn Arabic
    • Why you love travel
    • Your favorite hobby and why
Active Practice
  • Each day choose one theme (food, music, study, travel) and write 5 "I like X because Y" sentences.
  • Practice saying your opinion sentences out loud.
  • Create flashcards for opinion phrases and connectors.
  • Read your emotion diary entries aloud and record yourself.
Assignments

Assignment 1: Opinion Paragraph

  • Write 8–10 sentences on a topic you care about (e.g., why you want to learn Arabic, or why you love travel).
  • Include at least 3 opinion expressions.
  • Use لأن (because) at least twice.
  • Topics to consider:
    • Why I am learning Arabic
    • My favorite place to travel
    • What I love about Lebanese/Saudi culture
    • My preferred way to study

Assignment 2: Emotion Diary

  • For 5 days, write 2 sentences in Arabic about how you feel and why.
  • Use emotion vocabulary you've learned.
  • Connect your feelings to specific reasons using لأن.
  • Example: أشعر بالسعادة اليوم لأنني تعلمت كلمات جديدة — I feel happy today because I learned new words.
Quiz / Checkpoint
  • Fill-in-the-blank: pick the correct connector/phrase to complete 10 opinion sentences.
  • Match sentence halves: connect cause and effect (10 pairs).
  • Translate 5 opinion sentences from English to Arabic.
  • Read your opinion paragraph aloud and record yourself.
Culture & Reflection

Discuss in English: "How do Arabic poems you saw this week talk about love, place, or longing? Do you notice any recurring images (home, land, sea, etc.)?"

  • Read or watch analysis of Mahmoud Darwish's poetry and emotional language.
  • Note how Arabic expresses longing, love, and connection to place.
  • Compare emotional expression in Arabic poetry to poetry in your native language.
  • Reflect: "How does expressing opinions and emotions in Arabic feel different from English or Spanish?"
End-of-Week Reflection
  • Which opinion phrases feel most natural to you?
  • How comfortable do you feel expressing your preferences in Arabic?
  • Can you smoothly connect ideas using لأن?
  • What emotions are easiest for you to express in Arabic? Which are hardest?
  • How does the emotional language in Arabic poetry compare to what you know from other languages?
